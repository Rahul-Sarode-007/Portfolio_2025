LRIT

Long-Range Identification and Tracking System

Overview

The Long-Range Identification and Tracking (LRIT) system is a mandatory satellite-based vessel tracking system established under SOLAS Chapter V. It enables flag states, port states, and coastal states to receive automatic position reports from vessels flying their flag or operating in their waters, enhancing maritime security and safety worldwide.

Technical Features

  • Automatic Position Reporting: Transmits vessel identity, position, date, and time every 6 hours
  • Secure Data Transmission: Encrypted communication channels ensure data integrity and confidentiality
  • Conformance Testing: Mandatory testing to verify system compliance with IMO performance standards
  • Tamper-Proof Design: Protected against unauthorized access and manipulation
  • Continuous Monitoring: 24/7 operational status with automatic failure alerts
  • Integration Capability: Works seamlessly with existing ship communication systems
