GMDSS

Global Maritime Distress and Safety System

Overview

The Global Maritime Distress and Safety System (GMDSS) is an internationally agreed-upon set of safety procedures, equipment, and communication protocols designed to increase safety and make it easier to rescue distressed ships, boats, and aircraft. GMDSS uses satellite and terrestrial radiocommunications to ensure that no matter where a ship is in distress, help can be summoned and coordinated.

Sea Areas & Equipment Requirements

Sea Area A1

VHF Coverage
(20-30 nm from coast)

Sea Area A2

MF Coverage
(Up to 100 nm)

Sea Area A3

INMARSAT Coverage
(70°N to 70°S)

Sea Area A4

Polar Regions
(HF Coverage)

Core GMDSS Components

  • VHF DSC (Digital Selective Calling): Channel 70 for distress alerting in Sea Area A1
  • MF/HF DSC: Medium and High Frequency distress alerting for extended range
  • INMARSAT Ship Earth Station: Satellite communication for distress, safety, and routine communications
  • EPIRB (Emergency Position Indicating Radio Beacon): 406 MHz satellite distress beacon (COSPAS-SARSAT)
  • SART (Search and Rescue Transponder): 9 GHz radar transponder for locating survival craft
  • NAVTEX: Automated reception of maritime safety information (MSI)
  • EGC (Enhanced Group Calling): Reception of maritime safety information via satellite

Functional Requirements

GMDSS Must Provide Capability For:

  • Ship-to-Shore Distress Alerting: Via satellite or terrestrial radiocommunications
  • Shore-to-Ship Distress Alerting: Relay of distress alerts to ships in the vicinity
  • Ship-to-Ship Distress Alerting: Communication between vessels in distress situations
  • SAR Coordination: Search and rescue coordination communications
  • On-Scene Communications: Between ship in distress and assisting units
  • Locating Signals: Transmission and reception of locating signals
  • Maritime Safety Information: Reception of navigational and meteorological warnings
  • General Communications: Ship management and operational communications
  • Bridge-to-Bridge: VHF communications for navigation safety

Regulatory Requirements

SOLAS Chapter IV

  • Applicability: All passenger ships and cargo ships ≥300 GT on international voyages
  • Implementation Date: Mandatory since February 1, 1999
  • Certification: Radio Survey and GMDSS Radio Maintenance Certificate required
  • Manning: GMDSS Radio Operator's Certificate (GOC/ROC) required
  • Maintenance: At-sea or shore-based maintenance arrangements mandatory
  • Battery Requirements: Reserve power for all GMDSS equipment

Benefits

  • Enhanced Safety: Automated distress alerting ensures rapid response to emergencies
  • Global Coverage: Communication capability anywhere in the world
  • SAR Coordination: Improved coordination between rescue authorities and vessels
  • Safety Information: Automatic reception of navigational and meteorological warnings
  • Regulatory Compliance: Meets SOLAS Chapter IV requirements
